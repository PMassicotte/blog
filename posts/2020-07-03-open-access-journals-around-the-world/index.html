Open access journals around the World

Author

Philippe Massicotte

Published

July 3, 2020

It is easy to say that there is an increasing interest in publishing in open access journals. There are now more and more peer-reviewed open access journals where scientific research can be disseminated. That is why, as an academic, I recently decided to only review articles that are going to be published in open access journals. I will also strongly encourage the publication of my future articles in open access journals when I am the first author.

The Directory of Open Access Journal (DOAJ)

I recently discovered that the Directory of Open Access Journal provides a curated list of high-quality open access journals. At this time, there are 18048 journals indexed in their database. I found out that I could use this source of information to help me to find appropriate open access journals for my future research articles. DOAJ provides a list of their indexed journals as well as the countries in which they are published. I was interested to explore this data to know which countries publish open access journals. The following graph shows the first 20 countries listed in DOAJ in which open access journals are published. I was quite surprised to see that Indonesia was the country that publishes the most open access journals.

Indonesia may be the world’s OA leader: the study found that 81% of ﻿20,000 journal articles published in 2017 with an Indonesia-affiliated author are available to read for free somewhere online, and 74% are published with open-access licences, meaning they could be legally redistributed (see ‘Open-access leaders’). https://www.nature.com/articles/d41586-019-01536-5

The big five publishers

According to a paper published in 2015, Elsevier, Springer, Wiley, Taylor & Francis Group and SAGE Publishing are the main publishers that control more than half of the market of scientific. In the past few years, many universities and institutions have started to boycott these big publishers. Using the DOAJ’s data, I was interested to see if how these publishers embraced the open access movement. Not too surprisingly, we can see a peak in the creation of open access journals between 2013 and 2015.
